YouTube

YouTube is a large, media-rich video platform used for streaming, discovery, and content hosting. It delivers videos, channels, recommendations, and account-based features.

  • Contrast — YouTube generally uses strong contrast between text and backgrounds for readability, and video thumbnails use high-contrast imagery so content stands out.
  • Repetition — The site applies consistent patterns (thumbnail grid, player chrome, channel headers) across pages which helps users form expectations.
  • Alignment — Elements are well-aligned in predictable grid layouts; player controls and metadata are consistently placed which supports scanning and interaction.
  • Proximity — Related controls (title, channel, metadata, subscribe button) are grouped close to the player; recommendations are visually separated so users can distinguish primary content from related items.

Assessment: YouTube follows the CRAP principles closely — strong contrast and consistent repetition make content easy to scan. Alignment and proximity are well-handled in the player and listing pages. Its scale and personalization introduce some complexity, but overall the design supports user goals effectively.

Friv

Friv is a miscellaneous game-portal site that serves many lightweight browser games via a simple tile interface and lots of third-party content. The visual presentation is cluttered and inconsistent.

  • Contrast — Contrast is often low or chaotic, with many colorful tiles and backgrounds competing for attention which can make it hard to focus.
  • Repetition — Repetition is limited; different tiles and external embeds use widely varying styles, so patterns are hard to learn.
  • Alignment — Alignment is inconsistent: advertising, game tiles, and text blocks frequently collide or are offset, producing a scattered layout.
  • Proximity — Related items (game title, play button, ads) are sometimes separated or mixed with unrelated content, which reduces clarity about what to click or expect.

Assessment: Friv largely fails the CRAP principles — low/competing contrast, poor repetition, inconsistent alignment, and mixed proximity make it hard for users to quickly understand or trust the interface. The site feels noisy and crowded as a result.
